Implementation Roadmap: Making It Happen

Transform these recommendations into reality with specific, achievable actions designed for immediate impact.

Priority 1: Fix What's Broken (Next 30 Days)

1

AIPP Emergency Response Plan

Why urgent: 67% dissatisfaction rate is damaging artist trust

  • Week 1: Host emergency listening session with 10-15 recent AIPP applicants
  • Week 2: Publish transparent scoring rubric with real examples
  • Week 3: Launch "AIPP Office Hours" - weekly 2-hour drop-in sessions
  • Week 4: Release jury feedback template for all future decisions

Resources needed: 1 staff coordinator, $2K for facilitator, meeting space

2

Grant Concierge Pilot

Problem solving: 42% of first-timers abandon applications mid-process

  • Recruit: 2 past successful grantees as paid mentors ($25/hr, 10 hrs/week)
  • Services: Application review, budget template help, post-award check-ins
  • Schedule: Tuesday/Thursday virtual office hours + by appointment
  • Success metric: Reduce dropout rate to under 20% within 90 days

3-month pilot budget: $6,000 (can use existing professional development funds)

Priority 2: Equity Adjustments (Next 60 Days)

3

Thrive Match Requirement Overhaul

Current barrier: 37% of orgs under $250K can't meet 50% match

  • Immediate: Waive match for organizations under $100K budget
  • Sliding scale: $100-250K = 25% match, $250K+ = current 50%
  • Count as match: Volunteer hours ($29.95/hr), in-kind donations, space
  • Communication: Email all past declined applicants about new policy

Implementation: Policy memo to Council, update guidelines, train staff (2 weeks)

4

Elevate Application Simplification

Fixing: "Tourism metrics" language alienating community artists

  • Reframe: Replace "tourism impact" with "community vitality metrics"
  • New measures: Local attendance, repeat visitors, neighborhood partnerships
  • Simplify: Cut application from 12 to 6 pages, pre-fill repeat applicant data
  • Add: Video submission option for artists who struggle with writing

Timeline: Draft by Week 3, stakeholder review Week 4-5, launch Week 6

Priority 3: Awareness Campaign (Next 90 Days)

5

CSAP Relaunch: "Space Matters Austin"

Opportunity: High need + satisfied users = marketing problem, not program problem

  • Rebrand: New name, logo, and "Space Matters" campaign
  • Quarterly pop-ups: Host info sessions at makerspaces in Districts 3 & 1
  • Partner: Austin Creative Alliance, E3 Alliance for outreach
  • Simplify: One-page application, 30-day turnaround, $100K cap

Launch kit: $5K for materials, staff time for 4 events, partner MOUs

6

Heritage Preservation Visibility Boost

Hidden gem: 81% satisfaction but only 31% awareness

  • Target: Email every venue/org that mentioned "history" in survey
  • Create: "Heritage Heroes" social media series - past grantee stories
  • Partner: Preservation Austin, Mexican American Cultural Center
  • Bilingual: All materials in Spanish, Vietnamese, Arabic

Quick start: Intern project, use existing photo assets, $2K translation
